# boilerhaus code of ethics

## Purpose
- To define the principles that guide boilerhaus engineers, operators, partners, and AI agents in designing and deploying agentic systems. Ensure safety, integrity, and usefulness.

## Core values
- Safety first: protect users, data, and environment; design guardrails.
- Transparency and accountability: decisions and processes are visible; maintain decision logs.
- Humility and continuous learning: recognize limits of AI, seek feedback, learn from failures.
- Respect for privacy and consent: data minimization, consent, privacy-by-design.
- Fairness and non-discrimination: address bias; inclusive design.
- Stewardship of trust: maintain trust with users and stakeholders; avoid manipulation.
- Practical usefulness: build products people will actually use.

## Principles
- Do no harm: minimize risk; implement fail-safes; abort when risk is unacceptable.
- Guardrails by design: safety constraints built into systems; no ad hoc bypass.
- Human-in-the-loop: critical decisions require human oversight or review.
- Evidence-driven: decisions based on data; log and justify decisions.
- Ethical use of emerging tech: disclose capabilities and limitations; avoid harmful misuse.
- Privacy-by-design: minimize data collection; secure storage; access controls.
- Security-minded: protect against common vulnerabilities; respond to incidents.
- Accountability: clear ownership; auditable trails; regular reviews.
- Continuous governance: policies updated as technology and context evolve.

## Practices
- Development: include ethics review in design docs; perform risk assessments; maintain threat models; keep an auditable decision log.
- Data: use synthetic data where possible; minimize PII; encryption at rest/in transit; retention schedules.
- AI/testing: red-team, fault injection, chaos testing; toxicity checks; guardrails tested pre-deployment; kill-switch readiness.
- Deployment: stage gates; monitored metrics; attribution of decisions; ability to rollback.
- Disclosure: communicate limitations; disclaimers in user-facing interfaces.
- Conflicts of interest: disclose relationships; avoid biased design/deployment decisions.
- Incident handling: defined channels; post-incident reviews; public reporting if necessary.

## Governance
- Roles and responsibilities: owners, reviewers, and operators.
- Cadence: quarterly ethics review; after major incidents or releases; maintain versioned doc.

## Auditing and improvement
- Documentation: maintain source-of-truth docs; change logs; decision logs.
- Feedback loops: solicit user and stakeholder feedback; publish lessons learned.
- Training: ongoing ethics education; update onboarding.
