# Boilerhaus Federal Incorporation Guide

## Purpose

Create a practical checklist and setup guide for incorporating Boilerhaus federally, with `ScopeHouse` run as a product or operating name under Boilerhaus.

This is a founder guide.
It is not legal or tax advice.

## Recommended setup

Use a federal business corporation for the legal entity.

Recommended structure:

- legal entity: `Boilerhaus Inc.` if available
- fallback path: a numbered federal corporation if speed matters more than branding
- product or operating name: `ScopeHouse`

Why this direction:

- federal incorporation fits a company intended to operate beyond one province
- the name search for a named federal corporation is integrated into the online incorporation flow, so a separate federal Nuans purchase is usually not needed first
- Boilerhaus can remain the legal company while `ScopeHouse` stays the product brand

## Fast decision

### Option A, recommended

- incorporate as a named federal corporation
- use `Boilerhaus Inc.` or a close variant if available
- register `ScopeHouse` in Ontario if the company will identify itself to the public there under that name

Best when:

- you want the company brand and product brand kept separate
- you want the cleaner long-term umbrella company structure now

### Option B, speed-first

- incorporate a numbered federal corporation
- start operations with the numbered entity
- amend the legal name later once the final naming path is settled

Best when:

- you need the company formed quickly
- branding is less important than getting the corporation active

## Phase 1, lock the filing inputs

Decide these before starting the online application:

- final legal name and one fallback
- whether you will file named or numbered
- registered office province and full address
- first directors
- initial officers
- share structure and founder ownership split
- whether `ScopeHouse` will be used publicly in Ontario

Practical note:

The registered office is also where the corporate records are kept unless you make another compliant arrangement.

## Phase 2, prepare the filing

Have these ready:

- working legal name or numbered-corporation path
- payment method
- full registered office address in Canada
- first board of directors details
- articles details, including share classes and any restrictions
- working email address for confirmations

Naming note:

Corporations Canada says the name search is integrated into most CBCA online incorporation applications.
Do not buy a separate federal Nuans report first unless you specifically need name pre-approval or another separate service.

## Phase 3, file the federal incorporation

Use the Corporations Canada Online Filing Centre.

Current official fee:

- online incorporation: $200

Save immediately:

- certificate of incorporation
- articles of incorporation
- filing receipt
- confirmation emails

## Phase 4, complete post-incorporation records

Early after incorporation, run the organizational step for the corporation.

Corporations Canada says the directors can use the organizational meeting to:

- make bylaws
- adopt forms of share certificates and corporate records
- authorize the issue of shares
- appoint officers
- make banking arrangements

For Boilerhaus, treat these as immediate setup tasks:

- adopt bylaws
- appoint officers
- issue founder shares
- create the minute book or records folder
- create the ISC register
- file ISC information with Corporations Canada

Important ongoing rule:

Federal corporations now have to file ISC information annually with the annual return and within 15 days of a change.

## Phase 5, Ontario operating-name and registry requirements

Federal incorporation does not automatically answer every Ontario operating requirement.

Ontario says businesses with employees, facilities, or offices in Ontario must register using the Ontario Business Registry.

If Boilerhaus will carry on business in Ontario, confirm what Ontario Business Registry filings apply to the corporation and set up the Ontario Business Account access needed to manage them.

If Boilerhaus will identify itself to the public in Ontario as `ScopeHouse` rather than only as `Boilerhaus Inc.`, register `ScopeHouse` as the business name before using it publicly.

Do not assume that because the corporation is federal, the Ontario-facing operating-name steps disappear.

## Phase 6, CRA and tax setup

After incorporation:

- add the business number to CRA My Business Account
- confirm the corporation income tax account is visible
- decide whether to register for GST HST immediately or later
- decide whether a payroll account is needed before any salary or wage payments

Working tax notes:

- the CRA small-supplier threshold is generally $30,000 of taxable revenue over four consecutive calendar quarters, with separate timing rules if you exceed the threshold in a single quarter
- you can voluntarily register for GST HST earlier if it makes sense
- open a payroll account before paying salary, wages, bonuses, vacation pay, or other employment income

## Phase 7, annual compliance reminders

- file the annual return with Corporations Canada within 60 days of the corporation's anniversary date
- remember that the annual return is separate from the corporate tax return
- update ISC information annually and within 15 days of relevant changes
- keep director, address, and records changes current

## Suggested founder order of operations

1. confirm the legal name, fallback path, directors, and share structure
2. file federal incorporation online
3. save all incorporation outputs immediately
4. complete bylaws, officers, share issuance, and ISC setup
5. confirm and complete Ontario operating-name or registry steps for `ScopeHouse`
6. add CRA access and document GST HST and payroll decisions
7. open banking and bookkeeping under Boilerhaus
8. move contracts, invoices, and domains under the corporation

## Minimal document pack to save

Save these in one secure records location:

- certificate of incorporation
- articles of incorporation
- filing receipt
- bylaws
- director and officer resolutions
- share issuance records
- ISC register and related filing confirmations
- Ontario business-name or registry confirmations, if applicable
- CRA business number and tax account confirmations
- GST HST confirmation, if applicable
- payroll confirmation, if applicable
- bank account opening documents
